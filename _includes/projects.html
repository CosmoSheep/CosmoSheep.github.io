    Identifying Non-relativistic Dark Matter Models in Direct Detection with Machine Learing Method
    • Supervisor: Zuowei Liu, August 2017 - Now

    Advised by Prof. Zuowei Liu, I did some basic calculation about direct detection of dark matter, such as event rate taking account of annual modulation, Chi square scan. See my codes below about the details and results of my calculation. Then I read a paper analysing the possibility of distinguishing different dark matter models(arXiv: 1612.07808) if we hava direct detection experiment data. Meanwhile, I was auditing a machine learning (ML) course at Nanjing University, so the idea of using ML to classify physics models from large data interested me naturally. Then I studied non-relativistic effective theory of dark matter detection ((arxiv:1008.1591) in order to understand the models we want to differentiate in direct detection. Currently, I am simulating direct detection data based on different non-relativistic dark matter models.
